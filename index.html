Who I am

I'm an Operations and IT Project Manager who has spent the last few years building systems and delivering outcomes that actually work, not just on paper, but in the day-to-day reality of fast-moving teams.

My career has moved from establishing operational foundations from scratch to formally training in IT Project Management, where I stepped into a live product environment and led delivery from the ground up. Operations taught me what good execution looks like from the inside. Project Management gave me the framework to lead it at scale.

I work closely with decision-makers, build systems that reduce friction, and keep delivery momentum even when things get complicated. I bring discipline, strong judgment, and a people-first approach to every layer of the work.

How I Manage Projects

01

Clarify the Outcome

Define scope, timeline, and success metrics before a single task is assigned. Ambiguity at the start becomes a crisis at the end.

02

Structure the Work

Break projects into clear deliverables and assign ownership. Everyone should know what they own and when it's due.

03

Align the Team

Establish communication cadence and shared visibility. A team that knows what's happening doesn't need to be chased.

04

Maintain Momentum

Resolve blockers, manage dependencies, and keep stakeholders informed. Delivery momentum is protected, not assumed.
